Pyramex vs MSA Hard Hat — Which Brand Should You Buy?

Pyramex and MSA are two of the most widely used hard hat brands in the United States. Both meet ANSI/ISEA Z89.1 standards, both have full brim options, and both have loyal followings on job sites across every trade. So which one is actually better?

The honest answer depends on what you prioritize. Here's a direct comparison across the factors that matter most to workers who wear a hard hat all day.

Weight and Comfort

The Pyramex Ridgeline full brim weighs approximately 12.6 oz — one of the lightest full brim hard hats on the market. The MSA V-Gard full brim comes in around 14.4 oz. That 1.8 oz difference sounds small but is noticeable by hour six of a shift.

Both hats use a ratchet suspension system that adjusts at the back. The Pyramex Ridgeline suspension sits slightly higher off the skull, which many workers find more comfortable in hot weather because it allows more airflow. MSA's Fas-Trac suspension is widely regarded as one of the best in the industry — smooth ratchet action and very secure fit.

Weight (full brim)Pyramex Ridgeline: ~12.6 ozMSA V-Gard: ~14.4 oz
Suspension6-point ratchetFas-Trac III ratchet
AirflowHigher shell clearanceStandard clearance

Protection and Certification

Both the Pyramex Ridgeline and MSA V-Gard are ANSI/ISEA Z89.1 certified. Both are available in Type I and Type II configurations, and both offer Class C, G, and E electrical ratings depending on the specific model. Neither has a meaningful advantage over the other in terms of certified protection — both exceed the minimum standard by a comfortable margin in third-party testing.

Price

The Pyramex Ridgeline consistently comes in $5-15 cheaper than the MSA V-Gard at retail across most distributors. Both are affordable relative to other PPE, but if you're outfitting a crew the Pyramex price advantage adds up quickly.

Retail price range (full brim)Pyramex Ridgeline: $18-28MSA V-Gard: $25-40

Availability and Accessories

MSA has been in the market longer and has a wider ecosystem of compatible accessories — face shields, hearing protection attachments, and winter liners designed specifically for the V-Gard platform. Pyramex has expanded its accessory lineup significantly in recent years but MSA still has the edge here for workers who need a full PPE kit built around their hard hat.

Which One Should You Buy?

For most workers — especially those prioritizing all-day comfort and value — the Pyramex Ridgeline is the better choice. It's lighter, less expensive, and meets the same ANSI standard. The weight difference alone makes it worth it for long shifts.

MSA makes sense if you need the Fas-Trac suspension specifically, if you're building out a full PPE kit using MSA's accessory ecosystem, or if your employer has standardized on MSA across the site.

Either way — a plain white Pyramex Ridgeline or a plain white MSA V-Gard both do the same job. The only real differentiator at that point is what's on the outside.
